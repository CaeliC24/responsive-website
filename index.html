The Missing Link: Inflammation & Neuroplasticity

Chronic pain does not simply live in joints, discs, or nerves. It lives in the environment those nerves exist within. In persistent pain states, the brain’s immune cells, called microglia, become chronically activated.

Instead of acting as quiet caretakers, these cells release inflammatory chemicals that sensitize pain pathways, amplify stress responses, and impair clarity. When this inflammatory state persists, the nervous system becomes hypersensitive.

In that environment, forming new habits, retraining movement, or rewiring pain pathways becomes biologically difficult. The NeuroReset Program addresses this biological terrain first — creating stability so neuroplastic recovery can take root.

Why Brain Inflammation Is Often Overlooked

Pain Is Treated Like a Wiring Problem

Most approaches focus on structural findings or nerve blocks. But if the central nervous system remains inflamed, it continues amplifying signals regardless of local treatments.

Inflammation Is Invisible

Neuroinflammation does not reliably appear on standard imaging. Patients are often told everything looks normal while their nervous system remains chemically hypersensitized.

It Affects Mood, Energy & Clarity

Central inflammation contributes to fatigue, brain fog, stress sensitivity, and depressive symptoms — reinforcing the pain cycle from multiple angles.
